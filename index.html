About the Game

OLLIM is a 2D side-scrolling Metroidvania game developed as a GAM250 class project at DigiPen Institute of Technology. The player takes on the role of a boy who once guarded the stars. Upon receiving a divine command to save the twelve zodiac constellations that have fallen to the earth, he sets off on an adventure across the surface world.

The game features a meticulously designed Metroidvania progression: defeating each boss grants new abilities that unlock exploration of the next area. Players wield a unique starlight-reflection mechanic, fighting fluidly without ever attacking directly, using reflected light to redeem each fallen constellation and restore it to the sky.

HOW TO PLAY

Left-click to reflect starlight back at enemies. Observe and dodge incoming starlight attacks while staying aware of dangerous platforms beneath your feet. Each boss fight demands careful observation, precise timing, and mastery of the reflection mechanic to prevail.

Boss Spotlight

TAURUS — THE BULL

Once a radiant constellation adorning the night sky, Taurus lost most of its starlight to a mysterious, unknown force and plummeted to the earth. Consumed by confusion and pain, it now lashes out at anything that draws near, unleashing volleys of residual starlight at the player.

To redeem Taurus, the player must reflect its own starlight back, gradually restoring the brilliance it has lost and guiding this fallen constellation back to the heavens.

This Is Not Your Typical Action Game

In OLLIM, you never attack. Instead, you reflect starlight — turning every enemy's power against them in a combat system unlike anything you have played before. Built from the ground up on a custom OpenGL engine, OLLIM fuses Metroidvania exploration with intense boss rush encounters where survival depends on observation, timing, and reflexes rather than brute force. If you are looking for a fresh take on 2D action that challenges the conventions of the genre, OLLIM is the story worth telling.
